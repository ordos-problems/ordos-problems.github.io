Short Statement

An EFX allocation of indivisible goods is one where any envy agent iii feels toward agent jjj vanishes after removing any single good from jjj's bundle. Does a complete EFX allocation always exist for four or more agents, even with additive valuations?

Open Problem

does a complete EFX allocation exist for every instance with n≥4n\ge 4n≥4 additive agents?

In words, any envy that agent iii feels toward agent jjj would vanish upon removing any single (positively valued) good from jjj's bundle. Exact complete EFX allocations are known to always exist for up to 3 agents with additive valuations. Question: does a complete EFX allocation exist for every instance with n≥4n\ge 4n≥4 additive agents?

What Is Known

For additive goods, exact EFX allocations are known to exist for at most three agents, by Chaudhury, Garg, and Mehlhorn. For four agents, Berger and coauthors prove an almost-complete EFX allocation in which at most one good is left unallocated. For general nnn, EFX with charity is known, meaning that fewer than nnn goods may be donated, and a 0.6180.6180.618-EFX allocation always exists. The open problem is whether a complete exact EFX allocation always exists for additive goods when n≥4n\ge 4n≥4.

Remaining open problem. does a complete EFX allocation exist for every instance with n≥4n\ge 4n≥4 additive agents?
