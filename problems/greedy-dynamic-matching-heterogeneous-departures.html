Short Statement

In dynamic matching with Poisson arrivals, exponential abandonment and rewards, does the LP-selected greedy policy of Arnosti and Simon always achieve the tight 1/21/21/2 ratio against the omniscient benchmark without any homogeneous-departure-rate assumption?

What Is Known

Collina et al. (2020) gave a 1/81/81/8 guarantee against the omniscient benchmark in the general heterogeneous-arrival/departure model. Arnosti and Simon prove a tight 1/21/21/2 guarantee for their LP-selected greedy policy under homogeneous departures, and for bipartite instances with same-side homogeneous departures; no online policy can guarantee more than 1/21/21/2 even in homogeneous-departure instances. Their simulations find no counterexample in heterogeneous instances, and they prove the conjecture for two types.
