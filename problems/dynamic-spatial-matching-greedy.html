Cost of the greedy algorithm for dynamic spatial matching

Short Statement

Demand and supply points arrive i.i.d. uniform in [0,1]d[0,1]^d[0,1]d and each demand is matched online to an available supply, minimizing expected average matched distance. The minimum achievable cost is known across dimensions; what is the expected cost of the natural greedy rule (match each arrival to its nearest available counterpart)?

Open Problem

Open problem: characterize the expected average matching cost of the \emph{standard greedy} algorithm - match each arriving demand unit to the nearest currently available supply unit (and vice versa in the fully dynamic model) - in the semi-dynamic and fully dynamic models, for each dimension ddd.

Statement & Model

Supply and demand units are located i.i.d. uniformly in [0,1]d[0,1]^d[0,1]d. Consider two online models: the \emph{semi-dynamic} model (a pool of NNN supply units is in place and NNN demand units arrive sequentially, each matched on arrival to a still-available supply unit), and the \emph{fully dynamic} model (both supply and demand units arrive over time and unmatched units wait in the system). The cost is the expected average Euclidean distance between matched pairs as a function of system size NNN (and any excess supply MMM). The nearest-neighbor distance Θ(N−1/d)\Theta(N^{-1/d})Θ(N−1/d) is a lower bound. Kanoria (2021) determines the \emph{minimum achievable} cost in essentially all regimes: Θ(N−1/2)\Theta(N^{-1/2})Θ(N−1/2) for d=1d=1d=1, Θ(log⁡N /N)\Theta(\sqrt{\log N}\,/\sqrt{N})Θ(logN​/N​) for d=2d=2d=2, and Θ(N−1/d)\Theta(N^{-1/d})Θ(N−1/d) for d≥3d\ge 3d≥3 (static / semi-dynamic), achieved by a Hierarchical Greedy algorithm. \textbf{Open problem:} characterize the expected average matching cost of the \emph{standard greedy} algorithm - match each arriving demand unit to the nearest currently available supply unit (and vice versa in the fully dynamic model) - in the semi-dynamic and fully dynamic models, for each dimension ddd. Is standard greedy within a constant (or polylogarithmic) factor of the minimum achievable cost, or is it strictly (polynomially) worse?

What Is Known

Kanoria (2021) gives tight bounds on the minimum achievable cost in essentially all regimes (via Hierarchical Greedy), and notes that 'a sharp analysis of the standard greedy algorithm is challenging even in the semi-dynamic setting, and analyzing greedy in the fully dynamic setting may be even harder.' For d=1d=1d=1 with excess supply M=ϵNM=\epsilon NM=ϵN, an O(log⁡3N/N)O(\log^3 N / N)O(log3N/N) upper bound on greedy's cost is known (Akbarpour, Alimohammadi, Li, Saberi 2021). The general behavior of standard greedy, especially in the fully dynamic model and for d=2d=2d=2, is open.
