Optimal dispatching to heterogeneous parallel servers (JSQ optimality fails)

Short Statement

For identical exponential servers, Join-the-Shortest-Queue is provably optimal. When servers have different service rates, JSQ is no longer optimal and no simple policy is known to exactly minimize mean response time. Characterizing the optimal dispatching policy for heterogeneous parallel servers is open.

Open Problem

Determine the exact optimal dispatching policy for heterogeneous parallel-server queues, especially beyond asymptotic or heuristic speed-aware rules.

What Is Known

Proven: for homogeneous exponential servers, JSQ stochastically minimizes the sorted queue-length process and minimizes E[sum f(QjQ_jQj​)] for increasing-convex f, hence mean response time (Winston 1977; Weber 1978 extends to service times with non-decreasing hazard rate). Mean-field/asymptotic: power-of-d gives the double-exponential tail improvement over random routing (Vvedenskaya-Dobrushin-Karpelevich 1996; Mitzenmacher 2001); JSQ is heavy-traffic optimal with many servers in the Halfin-Whitt regime (Eschenfeldt-Gamarnik 2018). Heterogeneous servers: JSQ is provably NOT optimal once rates differ; SED/JSED and speed-aware variants are asymptotically (heavy-traffic) optimal in several settings, but no policy is known to be exactly optimal for mean response time, and the optimal policy is believed to have a complicated threshold structure. Open: (a) the exact optimal dispatching policy for heterogeneous parallel servers (distinct μ_j), explicitly noted as open in the load-balancing literature and the SIAM Review survey (van der Boor et al. 2022); (b) even for identical servers, exact non-asymptotic optimality of JSQ for mean sojourn time under general service-time distributions is open.

Remaining open problem. Determine the exact optimal dispatching policy for heterogeneous parallel-server queues, especially beyond asymptotic or heuristic speed-aware rules.
