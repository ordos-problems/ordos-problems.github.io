Short Statement

For asymmetric metric TSP, the Held-Karp LP integrality gap is now known to be a constant, but the exact value is open: known examples force it to be at least 222, while the best proof only shows it is at most 222222.

Open Problem

Determine the exact integrality gap of the Held-Karp LP for asymmetric metric TSP, closing the gap between the lower bound 222 and the upper bound 222222.

Statement & Model

In asymmetric TSP, the distance from A to B need not equal the distance from B to A (but the triangle inequality still holds). The Held-Karp LP lower-bounds the optimal tour. What Is Known

Charikar, Goemans, and Karloff gave instances showing that the Held-Karp relaxation has integrality gap at least 222. Svensson, Tarnawski, and Vegh proved the first constant upper bound, 319319319, resolving the earlier question of whether the gap was bounded at all. Traub and Vygen later improved the upper bound to 222222. The exact value is still unknown, and the large separation between 222 and 222222 suggests that either sharper rounding or stronger lower-bound constructions are needed.

Remaining open problem. Determine the exact integrality gap of the Held-Karp LP for asymmetric metric TSP, closing the gap between the lower bound 222 and the upper bound 222222.
