Short Statement

When sequencing customers with stochastic service times at a single server, is it optimal to order them by increasing variance (smallest-variance-first)?

Open Problem

is SVF sequencing optimal for general nnn (for exponential service times, and more generally under a suitable variability/dispersion ordering)?

Statement & Model

A single server must serve nnn customers (e.g. patients, surgeries) whose service times S1,…,SnS_1,\dots,S_nS1​,…,Sn​ are independent with known distributions. The customers are placed in a sequence and given appointment times; the cost is the expected weighted sum of customer waiting times and server idle time (and possibly overtime). For exponentially distributed service times, Wang (1999) proved that sequencing customers in increasing order of variance — equivalently increasing mean for exponentials — minimizes expected total cost for n=2n=2n=2, and conjectured optimality of this 'smallest-variance-first' (SVF) rule for all nnn. Question: is SVF sequencing optimal for general nnn (for exponential service times, and more generally under a suitable variability/dispersion ordering)?

What Is Known

Weiss (1990) introduced a dispersion measure, proved an ordering optimal for n=2n=2n=2, and disproved his measure's optimality at n=3n=3n=3. Wang (1999) established SVF optimality for n=2n=2n=2 with exponential service times and conjectured it for all nnn. Partial and asymptotic results and counterexamples in variants exist; the general-nnn optimality of SVF remains open.

Remaining open problem. is SVF sequencing optimal for general nnn (for exponential service times, and more generally under a suitable variability/dispersion ordering)?
