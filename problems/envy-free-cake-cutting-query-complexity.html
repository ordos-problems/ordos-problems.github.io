Query complexity of envy-free cake cutting

Short Statement

In the Robertson-Webb query model for envy-free cake cutting, a bounded protocol exists but uses a tower of six exponentials in nnn queries, while the best lower bound is only quadratic. What is the true query complexity?

Open Problem

Determine the true asymptotic growth of RW(n)\mathrm{RW}(n)RW(n); even an elementary (tower-free) upper bound or a super-quadratic lower bound is open.

Statement & Model

In cake cutting, a divisible good (the cake, modeled as [0,1][0,1][0,1]) is to be divided among nnn agents with general nonatomic additive valuations. In the Robertson-Webb query model, a protocol learns agents' valuations only through 'cut' queries (asking an agent to mark a point giving a specified value) and 'evaluate' queries (asking an agent the value of a specified interval). An allocation is envy-free if no agent prefers another agent's piece to her own. Let RW(n)\mathrm{RW}(n)RW(n) be the worst-case number of Robertson-Webb queries required by any deterministic protocol that returns an envy-free allocation of [0,1][0,1][0,1] among nnn agents. A finite, bounded envy-free protocol exists for every nnn (Aziz-Mackenzie), but its query bound is a tower of six exponentials in nnn; the best lower bound is only quadratic. Known: RW(n)=Ω(n2)\mathrm{RW}(n)=\Omega(n^2)RW(n)=Ω(n2) and RW(n)≤nnnnnn\mathrm{RW}(n)\le n^{n^{n^{n^{n^{n}}}}}RW(n)≤nnnnnn. Determine the true asymptotic growth of RW(n)\mathrm{RW}(n)RW(n); even an elementary (tower-free) upper bound or a super-quadratic lower bound is open.

What Is Known

Procaccia proved the best general lower bound, Ω(n2)\Omega(n^2)Ω(n2) Robertson-Webb queries. Aziz and Mackenzie gave the first finite bounded protocol for every number of agents, but the upper bound is a tower of six exponentials in nnn. This is one of the largest known gaps in fair division; substantial progress is known only for special cases such as four agents. The open problem is to determine the true asymptotic query complexity, or even to obtain either a tower-free upper bound or a super-quadratic lower bound.

Remaining open problem. Determine the true asymptotic growth of RW(n)\mathrm{RW}(n)RW(n); even an elementary (tower-free) upper bound or a super-quadratic lower bound is open.
