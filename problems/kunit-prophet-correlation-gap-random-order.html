Short Statement

For kkk-unit prophet inequalities, the correlation-gap constant ckc_kck​ can be beaten when the algorithm freely chooses the arrival order. Under random order the best known bound is stuck exactly at ckc_kck​ for k>1k>1k>1 (only k=1k=1k=1 is beatable). The open problem is to extend the free-order techniques to random order for k>1k>1k>1.

What Is Known

For k=1k=1k=1, Correa and coauthors show that the correlation-gap constant can be beaten. For k>1k>1k>1 under random arrival order, the best known online-to-offline guarantees remain stuck at the correlation-gap value ckCGc_k^{\mathrm{CG}}ckCG​, as in the Arnosti-Ma analysis of static thresholds. The open problem is to beat ckCGc_k^{\mathrm{CG}}ckCG​ for any fixed k>1k>1k>1 under random order; even an arbitrarily small improvement would establish a new separation between random order and the correlation-gap barrier.
