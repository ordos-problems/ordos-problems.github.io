Short Statement

In network revenue management, the deterministic LP upper-bounds optimal revenue and re-solving heuristics achieve O(1)O(1)O(1) regret under non-degeneracy in the independent-demand case. For choice-based NRM, no policy is known to achieve constant (TTT-independent) regret without a non-degeneracy assumption, and the tight relaxation gap and best simple-policy regret are uncharacterized.

Open Problem

Find a tractable policy with O(1)O(1)O(1) regret for general choice-based network revenue management, including degenerate CDLP instances, or prove such regret is impossible.

What Is Known

Independent-demand quantity-based NRM: Talluri & van Ryzin (1998) proved DLP bid-price controls are asymptotically optimal; naive frequent re-solving incurs O(T)O(\sqrt{T})O(T​) loss. Jasin & Kumar (2012) gave a re-solving heuristic with O(1)O(1)O(1) loss under non-degeneracy. Bumpensanti & Wang (2020) achieve uniformly bounded O(1)O(1)O(1) regret independent of T and capacities via infrequent re-solving with probability thresholding, under non-degeneracy. Vera & Banerjee (2021) obtain constant regret for online allocation with finite types. For degenerate LP / continuous-value models, Jiang-Ma-Zhang (2025) prove O(log⁡2T)O(\log ^2 T)O(log2T) regret with only densities bounded away from 0 (O(log⁡T)O(\log T)O(logT) under a growth condition), removing non-degeneracy for that formulation; whether O(1)O(1)O(1) is achievable for general degenerate discrete instances is unsettled. Choice-based NRM: Liu & van Ryzin (2008) showed the CDLP and its decomposition heuristic are asymptotically optimal as demand/capacity scale; Jasin & Kumar (2012) handle choice but rely on non-degeneracy. The non-linear structure of choice makes the fluid relaxation non-linear, and no policy is currently proven to achieve constant (T-independent) regret for general/degenerate choice-based instances, nor is the tight relaxation gap characterized — the genuinely open piece as of 2024-2025.

Remaining open problem. Find a tractable policy with O(1)O(1)O(1) regret for general choice-based network revenue management, including degenerate CDLP instances, or prove such regret is impossible.
