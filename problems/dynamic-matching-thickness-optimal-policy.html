Short Statement

In a market where agents arrive and depart over time and can only be matched to compatible partners present at the same time, what is the optimal matching policy — and exactly how valuable is waiting to 'thicken' the market?

Open Problem

characterize the optimal dynamic matching policy and the exact achievable loss in the general model — beyond the restricted class and beyond the specific sparsity regime.

Statement & Model

Agents arrive to a market as a Poisson process of rate mmm; each ordered/unordered pair of present agents is compatible independently with a small probability (yielding a sparse random compatibility graph with average degree ddd); each agent, if not yet matched, departs after an independent Exp(1)\mathrm{Exp}(1)Exp(1) lifetime. A planner observes the compatibility graph over time (in the informed variant, also learns when an agent is about to depart) and decides when and whom to match (a match removes two compatible present agents). The objective is to minimize the long-run fraction of agents who perish unmatched (or expected waiting plus loss). Akbarpour–Li–Oveis Gharan (2020) show greedy matching is near-optimal without departure information, while a 'patient' waiting policy is much better with it, and characterize the optimal waiting threshold within a restricted policy class. Question: characterize the optimal dynamic matching policy and the exact achievable loss in the general model — beyond the restricted class and beyond the specific sparsity regime.

What Is Known

Greedy loses an O(1/d)O(1/d)O(1/d) fraction without departure info; with departure info, waiting reduces loss to O(e−d/d)O(e^{-d}/d)O(e−d/d)-type rates; the optimal policy is characterized only within a restricted class and a specific random-graph regime (Akbarpour–Li–Oveis Gharan, JPE 2020). The general optimal policy, exact loss, and behavior on structured (non-random) compatibility graphs are open and actively studied.

Remaining open problem. characterize the optimal dynamic matching policy and the exact achievable loss in the general model — beyond the restricted class and beyond the specific sparsity regime.
