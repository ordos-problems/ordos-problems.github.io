Exact optimality of the cμ/θ index rule for multiclass queues with abandonment

Short Statement

A server handles several customer classes that abandon while waiting. Without abandonment the cμc\mucμ rule is exactly optimal; with abandonment the cμ/θc\mu/\thetacμ/θ rule is asymptotically optimal in heavy traffic. Open: whether cμ/θc\mu/\thetacμ/θ - or any static priority/index rule - is exactly optimal for the discounted or average-cost problem, and under what conditions.

Open Problem

Characterize when a static priority or index rule, such as cμ/θc\mu/\thetacμ/θ, is exactly optimal for multiclass queues with abandonment.

What Is Known

Proven: (i) Without abandonment (θ_i=0), the cμ rule is exactly optimal for the single-server multiclass problem (Cox & Smith; Buyukkoc-Varaiya-Walrand 1985). (ii) With abandonment, the cμ/θ rule is asymptotically optimal in the many-server Halfin-Whitt regime (Atar-Giat-Shimkin 2010) and under ergodic/average cost (Atar-Giat-Shimkin 2011). (iii) For the EXACT problem, Down-Koole-Lewis (2011) show the classic cμ-type priority rule does NOT hold in general with abandonment, but an additional condition on the ordering of the θ_i is sufficient to recover a static priority rule. (iv) Larrañaga-Ayesta-Verloop (2014, 2015) derive Whittle/index heuristics (via a restless-bandit relaxation) that are asymptotically optimal and perform well numerically but are not claimed exactly optimal. Open: a full characterization of the exact optimal policy for the multiclass M/M/1+M (and M/M/N+M) abandonment scheduling problem under discounted or average cost; in particular it is open in general (beyond the special orderings of Down-Koole-Lewis) when a static priority / index rule such as cμ/θ is exactly optimal.

Remaining open problem. Characterize when a static priority or index rule, such as cμ/θc\mu/\thetacμ/θ, is exactly optimal for multiclass queues with abandonment.
