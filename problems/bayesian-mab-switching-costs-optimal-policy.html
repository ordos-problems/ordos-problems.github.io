What Is Known

Proven: With any positive switching cost the Gittins-index decomposition fails — no per-arm index policy is optimal in general (Banks-Sundaram 1994). Proven (partial structure): under an optimal policy one need only consider switching at stopping times attaining the incumbent's Gittins index or a candidate's 'switching index,' and an algorithm to compute that switching index exists (Asawa-Teneketzis 1996), but this does not give a global index policy. The general optimal-policy computation is widely regarded as intractable (Jun 2004 survey); the restless/Markov generalization is PSPACE-complete (Papadimitriou-Tsitsiklis 1999). Open: a clean structural characterization of the exact optimal policy for general arm state spaces, or a tractable policy with provable optimality-gap guarantees. (This Bayesian/discounted setting is distinct from the regret-minimization setting in the companion entry.)

Remaining open problem. Characterize the optimal policy for discounted Bayesian multi-armed bandits with positive switching costs, or give a tractable policy with provable optimality-gap guarantees.
