Short Statement

What is the exact computational complexity of evaluating/optimizing a two-stage stochastic linear program, and does it admit an efficient (e.g. fully polynomial randomized) approximation scheme?

What Is Known

Dyer–Stougie (2006) argued two-stage stochastic programming is #P-hard and multistage is PSPACE-hard under explicit independent distributions (a subsequent note by Hanasusanto–Kuhn–Wiesemann (2016) corrected/clarified parts of the two-stage argument). SAA enjoys exponential convergence of the probability of a bad solution in the sample size, with sample complexity polynomial in dimension and 1/ϵ21/\epsilon^21/ϵ2 for fixed classes (Kleywegt–Shapiro–Homem-de-Mello; Shapiro–Nemirovski). The precise complexity/approximability and tight sample bounds for general two-stage problems remain unsettled.
