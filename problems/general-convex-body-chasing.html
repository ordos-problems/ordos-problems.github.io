Short Statement

Convex bodies in Rd\mathbb{R}^dRd arrive online and after each you must move your point into the current body, paying distance moved. For general (arbitrary) convex bodies, is the optimal competitive ratio Θ(d)\Theta(\sqrt{d})Θ(d​)?

Open Problem

Determine the optimal competitive ratio for general convex body chasing; in particular, is it Θ(d)\Theta(\sqrt d)Θ(d​)?

Statement & Model

In convex body chasing, convex bodies K1,K2,⋯⊆RdK_1,K_2,\dots\subseteq\mathbb{R}^dK1​,K2​,⋯⊆Rd are revealed online one at a time. After each body KnK_nKn​ is revealed, the algorithm must choose a point xn∈Knx_n\in K_nxn​∈Kn​, paying movement cost ∥xn−xn−1∥\|x_n-x_{n-1}\|∥xn​−xn−1​∥. The competitive ratio is sup⁡∑n∥xn−xn−1∥OPT,\sup \frac{\sum_n\|x_n-x_{n-1}\|}{\mathrm{OPT}},supOPT∑n​∥xn​−xn−1​∥​, where OPT\mathrm{OPT}OPT is the optimal offline cost. The nested case (each body contained in the previous, K1⊇K2⊇⋯K_1\supseteq K_2\supseteq\cdotsK1​⊇K2​⊇⋯) is essentially settled at Θ(d)\Theta(\sqrt{d})Θ(d​). For general (arbitrary) convex bodies in Euclidean space, the best upper bound is O(min⁡(d,dlog⁡N))O(\min(d,\sqrt{d\log N}))O(min(d,dlogN​)) (where NNN is the number of bodies) and the lower bound is Ω(d)\Omega(\sqrt d)Ω(d​). Determine the optimal competitive ratio for general convex body chasing; in particular, is it Θ(d)\Theta(\sqrt d)Θ(d​)?

What Is Known

The Friedman-Linial conjecture (a finite ratio depending only on d exists) is resolved: Sellke and Argue-Gupta-Guruganesh-Tang achieve O(d)O(d)O(d). Nested chasing is essentially tight at Θ(d)\Theta(\sqrt{d})Θ(d​). For general convex body chasing in Euclidean space, the best upper bound is O(min(d, sqrt(d log N))) versus Ω(d)\Omega(\sqrt{d})Ω(d​); the exact ratio remains open.

Remaining open problem. Determine the optimal competitive ratio for general convex body chasing; in particular, is it Θ(d)\Theta(\sqrt d)Θ(d​)?
