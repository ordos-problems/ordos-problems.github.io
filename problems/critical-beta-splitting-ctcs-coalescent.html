Short Statement

What is the precise relationship between the infinite critical beta-splitting tree and the beta(2,1) coalescent?

Open Problem

Characterize the limiting continuous-time critical beta-splitting tree CTCS(∞\infty∞), and determine whether and how its exchangeable partition dynamics are represented by the beta(2,1) coalescent.

Statement & Model

In the critical beta-splitting model, a clade of size mmm is split into subclades of sizes iii and m−im-im−i with probabilities proportional to 1/(i(m−i))1/(i(m-i))1/(i(m−i)). Aldous and Janson construct a consistent family of continuous-time trees CTCS(nnn), n≥2n\ge 2n≥2, under the operation of deleting a random leaf and pruning. Consistency suggests an infinite exchangeable limit CTCS(∞\infty∞). The open problem is to give a complete probabilistic description of this infinite object and to explain its relation to the beta(2,1) coalescent: whether the same merger structure appears exactly, after a time change, in a marginal projection, or only through an asymptotic correspondence.

What Is Known

Aldous and Janson give an overview of the critical beta-splitting model, including a central limit theorem for leaf heights, a description of the fringe distribution seen from a random leaf, and a canonical embedding into the CTCS(nnn) continuous-time model. The remaining open part is structural: the infinite exchangeable limit is only partly understood, and the connection to the beta(2,1) coalescent is explicitly identified as a major unresolved issue.

Remaining open problem. Characterize the limiting continuous-time critical beta-splitting tree CTCS(∞\infty∞), and determine whether and how its exchangeable partition dynamics are represented by the beta(2,1) coalescent.
