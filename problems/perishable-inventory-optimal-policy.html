A simple optimal policy for perishable inventory with fixed lifetime ≥ 3

Short Statement

For periodic-review inventory of a product that expires after a fixed number of periods (≥3\ge 3≥3), is any simple ordering policy exactly or asymptotically optimal?

Open Problem

does there exist a simple, tractable policy (e.g. an order-up-to rule in total inventory, or a critical-number policy) that is exactly optimal for m≥3m\ge 3m≥3, or that is provably asymptotically optimal with explicit performance guarantees?

Statement & Model

Consider a single item under periodic review whose units perish exactly mmm periods after arrival (fixed shelf life), with i.i.d. demand, FIFO issuance, and linear holding, outdating (expiry), and shortage (or lost-sales) costs. The state needed for the dynamic program is the age profile of on-hand stock — the vector (x1,…,xm−1)(x_1,\dots,x_{m-1})(x1​,…,xm−1​) of quantities with 1,…,m−11,\dots,m-11,…,m−1 periods of remaining life — so the optimal order quantity is a function of this whole vector. For m≥3m\ge 3m≥3 the optimal policy is not of base-stock/order-up-to form and is computationally intractable (curse of dimensionality). Question: does there exist a simple, tractable policy (e.g. an order-up-to rule in total inventory, or a critical-number policy) that is exactly optimal for m≥3m\ge 3m≥3, or that is provably asymptotically optimal with explicit performance guarantees?

What Is Known

The two-period case m=2m=2m=2 is well understood (Nahmias–Pierskalla 1973). For m≥3m\ge 3m≥3 no simple optimal policy is known; Nahmias (1976) gives an effective approximate critical-number heuristic. Recent work proves asymptotic optimality of base-stock policies (as the outdating/penalty cost structure or lifetime grows) and of constant-order policies in certain regimes, but a simple policy that is exactly optimal, or asymptotically optimal with tight finite-horizon guarantees in general, remains open.

Remaining open problem. does there exist a simple, tractable policy (e.g. an order-up-to rule in total inventory, or a critical-number policy) that is exactly optimal for m≥3m\ge 3m≥3, or that is provably asymptotically optimal with explicit performance guarantees?
