Short Statement

In the heterogeneity-ball framework for data-driven decisions, can one efficiently compute or approximate general robust policies that retain the known rate-optimal worst-case regret guarantees?

Open Problem

The open problem is algorithmic: can these general robust policies be computed efficiently, or approximated in a way that preserves their rate-optimal regret guarantees, for broad classes of OR/MS problems beyond the worked examples?

Statement & Model

Consider the framework of Besbes, Ma, and Mouchtaki: a decision x∈Xx\in\mathcal{X}x∈X is evaluated on a future distribution μ\muμ, while historical observations are independent but may come from different unknown distributions ν1,…,νn\nu_1,\dots,\nu_nν1​,…,νn​ lying in a radius-ϵ\epsilonϵ heterogeneity ball around μ\muμ under a specified metric such as Kolmogorov, Wasserstein, total variation, or a divergence. A data-driven policy maps historical samples into a decision, and performance is measured by asymptotic worst-case expected regret relative to the oracle decision optimal for μ\muμ. SAA is rate-optimal for some problem/metric pairs but fails badly for others, such as pricing under Wasserstein heterogeneity and ski-rental variants. The paper proposes robust alternatives such as RDRO and DRO policies and proves rate-optimality in several settings. The open problem is algorithmic: can these general robust policies be computed efficiently, or approximated in a way that preserves their rate-optimal regret guarantees, for broad classes of OR/MS problems beyond the worked examples?

What Is Known

Besbes, Ma, and Mouchtaki give a formal heterogeneity-ball model, upper/lower bounds for SAA, and problem-dependent rate-optimal robust policies for examples including newsvendor, pricing, and ski rental. They explicitly leave open efficient computation of the RDRO/DRO policies, approximations that still perform well, and broader finite-sample/adaptive-to-heterogeneity guarantees.
