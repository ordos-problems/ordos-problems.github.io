Short Statement

For bin packing, how far can the integer optimum be from the value of the Gilmore-Gomory configuration LP relaxation in absolute (additive) terms? Is the gap always O(1)O(1)O(1) extra bins, or can it grow?

Open Problem

Determine whether the additive integrality gap is O(1)O(1)O(1) or Θ(log⁡)\Theta(\log)Θ(log).

Statement & Model

In the bin packing problem, items of given sizes must be packed into unit-capacity bins, minimizing the number of bins used. The standard strong LP relaxation is the Gilmore-Gomory configuration LP. For a bin packing instance III, let OPTf(I)\mathrm{OPT}_f(I)OPTf​(I) be the value of the Gilmore-Gomory LP relaxation and OPT(I)\mathrm{OPT}(I)OPT(I) the integer optimum. The question is whether gap=O(1)\mathrm{gap}=O(1)gap=O(1) (a constant number of extra bins regardless of instance). The related Modified Integer Round-Up Property (MIRUP) conjecture posits OPT(I)≤⌈OPTf(I)⌉+1\mathrm{OPT}(I)\le\lceil \mathrm{OPT}_f(I)\rceil+1OPT(I)≤⌈OPTf​(I)⌉+1. What Is Known

Hoberg and Rothvoss proved that the integer optimum is at most OPTf+O(log⁡OPTf)\mathrm{OPT}_f+O(\log \mathrm{OPT}_f)OPTf​+O(logOPTf​), improving the earlier O(log⁡2OPTf)O(\log^2 \mathrm{OPT}_f)O(log2OPTf​) additive bound of Karmarkar and Karp. On the lower-bound side, no family is known with additive gap growing beyond a constant, and the stronger MIRUP conjecture predicts an extremely small constant gap. The main open question is whether the true additive gap is bounded by O(1)O(1)O(1) or whether the logarithmic upper bound is qualitatively necessary.
