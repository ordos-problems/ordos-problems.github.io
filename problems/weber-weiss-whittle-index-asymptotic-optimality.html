Whittle index asymptotic optimality and the global attractor condition (Weber-Weiss)

Short Statement

Whittle (1988) conjectured his index policy is asymptotically optimal for restless bandits as the number of arms grows. Weber-Weiss (1990) proved this under indexability plus a global-attractor condition on the mean-field ODE, but gave a counterexample where it fails. Open: when the global-attractor/non-degeneracy conditions hold and the minimal assumptions for each optimality-gap rate.

Open Problem

Give checkable conditions under which Whittle's index policy is asymptotically optimal, and characterize when its optimality gap is O(N)O(\sqrt N)O(N​), O(1)O(1)O(1), or exponentially small.

What Is Known

Proven: (i) Weber-Weiss (1990) — indexability + a global attractor of the mean-field ODE implies WIP is asymptotically optimal, plus an indexable counterexample (stable limit cycle) where WIP is NOT asymptotically optimal, so Whittle's conjecture is false in general (a technical gap patched by the 1991 addendum). (ii) Gast-Gaujal-Yan (2023) — under indexability + uniform global attractor + a non-degenerate fixed point, the gap is O(e−cN)O(e^{-cN})O(e−cN); at a degenerate fixed point it can be only O(1/N)O(1/\sqrt{N})O(1/N​); with no global attractor WIP converges to a limit-cycle time-average and is generally suboptimal. (iii) Gast-Gaujal-Yan (Math of OR 2024) — necessary AND sufficient conditions for general LP/priority policies for each rate regime; an explicit LP-index policy is asymptotically optimal with O(1/N)O(1/\sqrt{N})O(1/N​) on ALL models, exponential under non-degeneracy + UGAP. (iv) Verloop (2016) gives asymptotic optimality of priority policies (incl. non-indexable) under a global attractor. (v) Hong-Xie-Chen-Wang removed the hard-to-verify assumptions: O(1/N)O(1/\sqrt{N})O(1/N​) without UGAP (FTVA 2023; unichain+aperiodicity 2025), and O(e−CN)O(e^{-CN})O(e−CN) under aperiodic-unichain + non-degeneracy + local stability with a matching lower bound (two-set policy 2024). Indexability itself is hard to verify; sufficient conditions exist only for structured families (Niño-Mora's partial conservation laws). Still open: a practically checkable characterization of the global-attractor/UGAP condition; the exact minimal assumptions per rate regime (esp. for index/priority policies); and whether indexability plus something easily verifiable can guarantee asymptotic (let alone exponential) optimality of the Whittle index policy specifically.

Remaining open problem. Give checkable conditions under which Whittle's index policy is asymptotically optimal, and characterize when its optimality gap is O(N)O(\sqrt N)O(N​), O(1)O(1)O(1), or exponentially small.
