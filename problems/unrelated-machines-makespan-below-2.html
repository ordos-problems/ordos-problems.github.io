Short Statement

For makespan minimization on unrelated machines, the classic Lenstra-Shmoys-Tardos algorithm gives a 222-approximation and it is NP-hard to beat 3/23/23/2. Whether a ratio below 222 (ideally 3/23/23/2) is achievable has been open since 1990.

Statement & Model

Given jobs and machines where each job takes a different (machine-dependent) time on each machine, assign every job to a machine to minimize the makespan (the load of the busiest machine). What Is Known

Lenstra, Shmoys, and Tardos gave the classic 222-approximation and also proved that no polynomial-time algorithm can achieve a ratio below 3/23/23/2 unless P=NP\mathrm{P}=\mathrm{NP}P=NP. Several structured special cases, including restricted assignment and graph balancing, admit better guarantees. For the full unrelated-machines problem, however, no polynomial-time approximation ratio strictly below 222 is known. The open problem is to beat the 222 barrier, or to prove a stronger hardness result that explains why the original [3/2,2][3/2,2][3/2,2] gap persists.
