Optimal policy structure for dual-sourcing inventory (fast/slow sources)

Short Statement

A firm replenishes a single product from a cheap slow source and an expensive fast source under stochastic demand and full backlogging. When the lead-time gap is at least 2, the optimal ordering policy's structure is unknown and believed intractable; whether any simple policy (Tailored Base-Surge, dual-index, single-index) is optimal is open.

Open Problem

Characterize the exact optimal policy for dual sourcing when the regular and expedited lead times differ by at least two periods.

What Is Known

For lead-time gap LrL_rLr​ - LeL_eLe​ = 1, the optimal expedited policy is base-stock and the problem is tractable; for gap >= 2 the optimal structure is unknown and the problem has 'resisted solution for over 40 years.' Heuristics perform well numerically but are not optimal in general: the dual-index policy (Veeraraghavan & Scheller-Wolf 2008) and new policy classes (Sheopuri-Janakiraman-Seshadri 2010); TBS analyzed by Janakiraman-Seshadri-Sheopuri 2015. The central proven result is that TBS is asymptotically optimal as LrL_rLr​ -> infinity with LeL_eLe​ fixed (Xin & Goldberg 2018); a 2026 working paper (Optimization Online) further shows the TBS optimality gap decays exponentially in LrL_rLr​. What remains open: the exact optimal policy for any finite gap >= 2; whether any single simple policy class is exactly optimal; and tight non-asymptotic guarantees for moderate LrL_rLr​ - LeL_eLe​. The Goldberg-Reiman-Wang (2021) survey presents dual-sourcing as a flagship case where only asymptotic characterizations are available.
