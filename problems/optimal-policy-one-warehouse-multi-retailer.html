Optimal policy for one-warehouse multi-retailer (distribution) inventory systems

Short Statement

A single warehouse ships, with positive lead times, to several retailers each facing independent random demand. The optimal replenishment-and-allocation policy is unknown: echelon base-stock is optimal only under the artificial balance assumption (which permits negative allocations); without it, the true optimal structure is open.

Open Problem

Characterize the optimal replenishment-and-allocation policy for one-warehouse multi-retailer inventory under the true nonnegative-allocation constraints.

What Is Known

Echelon base-stock policies are optimal ONLY under the balance/allocation assumption (Zipkin 1984; Federgruen-Zipkin 1984; Eppen-Schrage), which relaxes the no-lateral-transshipment constraint by permitting negative allocations. Under the true qiq_iqi​ >= 0 constraint, echelon base-stock is provably suboptimal and the exact optimal policy is uncharacterized. For the deterministic-demand lot-sizing OWMR variant, Levi-Roundy-Shmoys-Sviridenko (2008) gave a 1.8-approximation via LP rounding; power-of-two policies give ~1.26; recent FPTAS-based guarantees exist (Operations Research 2024). For the stochastic divergent problem, only heuristics with numerically-assessed gaps and asymptotic-optimality results (e.g., Lagrangian policies for lost-sales multi-store systems) are known. A clean structural characterization, or a simple policy with an explicit small constant-factor guarantee for the stochastic backlog objective, remains open as of 2024-2025.

Remaining open problem. Characterize the optimal replenishment-and-allocation policy for one-warehouse multi-retailer inventory under the true nonnegative-allocation constraints.
